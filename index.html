Intention setting for #BLM allies

My hope is that these questions will help you create a personal manifesto for how you want to show up as an ally, something you can feel anchored to and personally driven by to reduce feelings of burnout so you can keep doing sustainable work.

This year has been especially nuts and it's okay if you're not feeling ideal right now. Before you start reflecting, I invite you to find a safe and relatively quiet place if you have access to one, and take a minute to breathe slowly and deeply from your diaphragm until you feel more relaxed than you were before.

Examples
  • "I want to show up in a supportive way that helps others feel heard"
  • "I want to actively dismantle the ways I have been complicit in an unfair system"
  • "I want to help sway and onboard new allies"
  • "I want to contribute to structural equality around my city/neighborhood"
  • "I want to better align my actions with my ethical values"
Examples
  • "I will offer support without expecting recognition"
  • "I will seek out and amplify black perspectives"
  • "I will do my best to listen and fully believe, with an open mind, without interrupting"
  • "When I make mistakes, I will focus on making amends instead of centering my shame or discomfort"
  • "I will find calls to action made by black community leaders"
  • "I will share and discuss my learnings with other allies"
There's no shame in not having a skill right at this moment. We all start from wherever we are right now.)
Examples
  • "I will learn more about my privileges and what it's like for those who don't have them"
  • "I will learn how I can use my privileges in service of others' safety"
  • "I will read books and articles on how to be a better ally to BIPOCs"
  • "I will read about what BIPOCs want from allies so I'm not offering unsolicited actions"
  • "I will learn more about modern black history as told by black authors/speakers"
  • "I will learn what microaggressions look like"
  • "I will learn how to mindfully sit with my discomfort so I can be present for others"
  • "I will learn how to get involved in my local government"
Suggestions
  • Action / resource list
  • For Our White Friends Desiring To Be Allies | Sojo
    Suggestions on how to better hold space for BIPOCs
  • What is intersectionality? | The Conversation
    On intersectional privilege, and how to unpack different aspects of it
  • So You Want To Talk About Race (Ijeoma Oluo)
    On recognizing subtle everyday racism, tips for new allies, and structural injustice. Has clear bullet point guides on how to address race more productively in conversation.
  • The New Jim Crow: Mass Incarceration In An Age Of Colorblindness (Michelle Alexander)
    A historical compilation on the mechanisms of modern slavery and black oppression through statistically biased mass incarceration in America.
Ideas
  • go through a resource you listed above
  • find a protest, or a way to provide support/aid to protesters
  • call/text local legislators
  • find a volunteer opportunity
  • donate, or budget out your donation capacity
  • share resources with friends (or strangers)
  • ask someone who is having a difficult time if you can do anything for them
  • have an empathetic conversation with someone neutral to this movement, and win them over instead of driving them away
  • use your goodwill with a non-allied acquaintance or relative to initiate an incredibly uncomfortable empathetic conversation about the importance of this movement to you
  • make a list of actions to take

Personal anchors

(I don't mean "why is this movement important"; if we're here, we already know that people's lives are important for their own sake. Deep inside, what personal thing selfishly drives YOU to prioritize this?)
Examples
  • Your own PoC experience?
  • Personal trauma empathy, or empathy with feelings of disempowerment & danger?
  • Parallels with a family story?
  • Wanting people you care about personally to be safe? Experiences from BIPOCs who are close to you?
  • Rage about unfair systems?
  • Wanting to make a difference in something that matters?
  • Wanting to feel good and helpful?
  • Wanting to fix systems that are broken?
Examples
  • "Everyone deserves a safe home"
  • "It's just the right thing to do"
  • "We are all in this world together"
Examples

  • It is okay and normal to feel discomfort.
  • I did not choose this privilege. I am choosing to dismantle it.
  • It is important for me to fully understand my privilege so I can actively reduce the ways that systemic privilege harms others.
